# Doxa.Life Glossary Candidates — AI Analysis

---

## 1. People-group terminology

These are the most definitionally precise terms on the site. The
**Definitions** page gives exact numeric thresholds; translations must carry
those thresholds intact.

---

### People group ★ Seed term

- **Site definition**: "An ethnolinguistic group who perceive themselves to
  have a common affinity for one another based on language, culture, religion,
  and worldview … the largest possible group within which the gospel can spread
  as a viable, indigenous church-planting movement without encountering barriers
  of understanding or acceptance." (Definitions page)
- **Example**: "2,085 unengaged people groups" (homepage headline)
- **Why it matters**: This is the fundamental unit of measurement for the
  entire site. The concept is ethnic/linguistic, not political — "people group"
  must not be rendered as "nation-state", "tribe" (with primitive connotations),
  or "social group". An imprecise translation invalidates every statistic.

---

### Unreached people group (UPG) ★ Seed term

- **Site definition**: "Has approximately 2 disciples or fewer out of 100
  (≤ 2%) and lacks the capacity to establish indigenous churches without
  cross-cultural assistance." (Definitions page)
- **Example**: "Global Partnership for the Unreached" (site tagline)
- **Why it matters**: The threshold (≤ 2%) must be reproducible in the
  translation. The concept of *lack of capacity* for self-propagation is as
  important as the percentage. Avoid wording that implies the group is
  spiritually "unchosen" or socially marginal.

---

### Unengaged (people group) ★ Seed term

- **Site definition**: A people group with no known believers or a tiny few
  and lacking the four primary levels of effective engagement (apostolic effort
  in residence; commitment to the local culture and heart language; long-term
  ministry commitment; sowing the gospel aimed at a CPM). (Definitions page)
- **Example**: "2,085 unengaged people groups" / "Unengaged people groups have
  no missionaries, no churches, and often no known believers." (Adopt page)
- **Why it matters**: "Unengaged" is a technical term meaning *no active
  church-planting work*, not "socially unengaged" or "emotionally unresponsive".
  A mistranslation here obscures the entire urgency of the site's call to action.

---

### Unengaged unreached people group (UUPG)

- **Meaning**: The combined category — unreached AND lacking the four levels of
  effective engagement. The research page uses "Find a UUPG" as a UI label.
- **Example**: "Daily prayer for each of the 2,085 UUPGs" (Vision page)
- **Why it matters**: UUPG is an established acronym in global missions. Keep a
  stable equivalent acronym (e.g., "UUPG") or a consistently used phrase in
  each language to align with international prayer tools.

---

### Under-engaged (people group) ★ Seed term

- **Site definition**: "Has approximately 1 disciple or fewer out of 100
  (≤ 1%). More church planting teams are needed to increase fruitful
  engagement." (Definitions page)
- **Example**: "Fruitful engagement among frontier peoples and the
  under-engaged" (homepage)
- **Why it matters**: The hyphenated compound form appears consistently.
  Convey that work has begun but is *insufficient in scale* — not that the
  people themselves are indifferent.

---

### Frontier people (group) ★ Seed term

- **Site definition**: "Has approximately 1 disciple or fewer out of 1,000
  (≤ 0.1%) with no confirmed, sustained movement to Jesus. Often geographically
  isolated with little to no access to the gospel; pioneer work generally must
  begin with nonbelievers." (Definitions page)
- **Example**: "Fruitful engagement among frontier peoples" (homepage goal)
- **Why it matters**: "Frontier" signals the missiological cutting-edge —
  the most unreached of the unreached. Avoid military connotations where
  culturally sensitive; preserve the sense of work among them might be their "first-ever" gospel contact.

---

### Ethnolinguistic group

- **Meaning**: The technical descriptor of what constitutes a people group
  (shared language, culture, religion, worldview). Not used as a standalone
  label in the UI, but embedded in the Definitions page definition.
- **Why it matters**: Translators should understand this is the underlying
  concept so they choose ethnicity/language-oriented words, not political ones.

## 2. Engagement and mission language

---

### Engagement / engaged (people group)

- **Site definition** (Vision page — "What is Engagement?"): "There is
  sustained activity to share Christ and make disciples; there are efforts to
  establish self-sustaining churches; the work occurs in culturally appropriate
  and locally relevant ways." For WAGF: cross-cultural workers are resident.
- **Example**: "To engage every Unengaged People Group by 2033" (Priority)
- **Why it matters**: "Engagement" is a precision term — resident, sustained,
  cross-cultural, CPM-oriented. A vague rendering like "involvement" or
  "contact" strips the core meaning. The whole priority statement depends on it.

---

### Fruitful engagement

- **Meaning**: Engagement that is visibly producing disciples and churches,
  not just activity. Used in homepage goals and the About page declaration.
- **Example**: "Fruitful, long-term, residential engagement of the world's
  unengaged peoples" (Sending Summit Declaration)
- **Why it matters**: The qualifier "fruitful" is theologically and
  strategically intentional — it distinguishes activity from results.

---

### Gospel access ★ Seed term

- **Meaning**: The condition of a people group having the gospel available to
  them — through workers, churches, or gospel content. Its *absence* is the
  defining tragedy of unengaged people groups.
- **Example**: "They have no gospel access" (Adopt page heading); "A
  church-led commitment to pray, give, and send so that gospel access begins"
  (Adopt page)
- **Why it matters**: "Access" implies availability and reachability, not just
  geographic presence. Mistranslating as "gospel knowledge" or "gospel hearing"
  changes the meaning subtly but importantly.

---

### Gospel engagement ★ Seed term

- **Meaning**: Active, ongoing proclamation and living-out of the gospel
  among a people group; used in the prayer context ("prepare the way for gospel
  engagement through prayer").
- **Example**: "Help prepare the way for gospel engagement through prayer"
  (Pray page)
- **Why it matters**: Distinct from "gospel access" (availability) —
  "gospel engagement" is the active missional work itself. Avoid rendering as
  a marketing or media campaign.

---

### Church-planting movement (CPM) ★ Seed term

- **Site definition**: "A multiplication of indigenous churches planting
  churches that includes at least four streams of four spiritual generations
  together totaling more than 1,000 persons." (Definitions page)
- **Example**: "Church planting movements among every unreached people on
  earth" (homepage goal)
- **Why it matters**: "Movement" implies self-multiplying momentum, not a
  single church plant or program. The metrics (4 streams × 4 generations ×
  1,000 persons) must be translatable; avoid wording that sounds like a social
  or political movement.

---

### Indigenous church

- **Meaning**: A church arising from within the people group that is
  self-sustaining and maturing in the "five selfs".
- **Example**: "There is an indigenous church planted among every people group
  on earth" (Vision goals); "lacks the capacity to establish indigenous churches
  without cross-cultural assistance" (Definitions)
- **Why it matters**: "Indigenous" means locally rooted and self-governing,
  not "native religion" or "animist". Clarify if necessary to avoid syncretism
  associations.

---

### The five "selfs" (of an indigenous church)

- **Meaning**: The five markers of a mature indigenous church:
  self-propagating, self-governing, self-supporting, self-theologizing,
  self-missionizing. (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: This is a standard missions framework. Translators should
  use established equivalents if they exist in the target language; if not,
  each "self-" compound needs a consistent rendering.

---

### Residential engagement / workers in residence

- **Meaning**: Cross-cultural workers who *live among* the people group
  (not remote or visiting). A requirement for WAGF's definition of engagement.
- **Example**: "Cross-cultural workers are resident" (Vision page);
  "Apostolic (pioneering) effort in residence" (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: "Resident" versus "nonresident" is a key distinction on
  the site. Translations must not flatten this to simply "missionaries".

---

### Cross-cultural workers

- **Meaning**: Mission workers from a different culture serving among a people
  group. Used as the preferred term over "missionary" in some contexts.
- **Example**: "lacks the capacity to establish indigenous churches without
  cross-cultural assistance" (Definitions)
- **Why it matters**: The "cross-cultural" qualifier is intentional — these are
  not workers from within the group. Use a term that conveys cultural difference,
  not just "foreign".

---

### Apostolic (pioneering) effort

- **Meaning**: Front-edge mission work that begins where there are no churches
  or believers. One of the four criteria for a people group being "engaged".
- **Example**: "Apostolic (pioneering) effort in residence" (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: "Apostolic" here means sent/pioneering, not a
  denominational title. The parenthetical "(pioneering)" signals this; retain
  that clarity in translation.

---

### Pioneer work

- **Meaning**: The initial evangelism phase among a frontier people group
  where there are no believers to begin with.
- **Example**: "Pioneer work generally must begin with nonbelievers"
  (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: Translators should convey "starting from scratch" in
  terms of gospel presence.

---

### Heart language

- **Meaning**: A person's first/mother tongue — the language in which they
  think and feel most deeply.
- **Example**: "Commitment to work in the local culture and heart language"
  (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: A well-established missions phrase. Distinguish from
  "official language" or "trade language". The concept is that gospel and
  discipleship work must happen in this language to be fully effective.

---

### Movement to Jesus

- **Meaning**: A collective, multiplying movement of people from a group
  turning to follow Jesus — as opposed to isolated conversions.
- **Example**: "No confirmed, sustained movement to Jesus" (frontier people
  group definition)
- **Why it matters**: This is specifically about a *sustained, multiplying*
  spiritual movement, not individual conversion or social activism.

---

### Nonresident partners

- **Meaning**: Churches, agencies, or individuals who support engagement from
  outside the field — through prayer, giving, Bible translation, media, etc.
  Broader engagement includes important contributing components that can be done
  by nonresident partners.
- **Example**: "Broader engagement includes important contributing components
  that can be done by nonresident partners, including intercessory prayer, Bible
  translation, compassion/disaster ministry outreaches…" (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: Helps people understand they can participate in mission
  even without going to the field.

---

### Broader engagement (components)

- **Meaning**: The contributing (but not sufficient alone) elements of mission
  that nonresident partners can do: intercessory prayer, Bible translation,
  compassion/disaster ministry, media reach, mobilization efforts, leadership
  training, work among diaspora.
- **Example**: "Broader engagement includes important contributing components"
  (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: This list clarifies that adoption and prayer are genuine
  participation in the mission, even without going.

---

### Diaspora

- **Meaning**: Members of a people group living outside their homeland.
  Listed as a component of broader engagement.
- **Example**: "Work among diaspora" (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: Often a loanword in many languages; if so, it may be
  kept. Clarify it means *scattered communities* of a people group, not
  refugees in general.

## 3. Prayer and intercession

---

### Daily prayer (for a people group) ★ Seed term

- **Meaning**: Committing to pray for a specific unengaged people group every
  day, typically using the daily prayer guide from Doxa.Life.
- **Example**: "Choose an unengaged people group you will commit to pray for
  daily." (Pray page)
- **Why it matters**: "Daily" is intentional and part of the 24-hour coverage
  math. Avoid rendering as "regular prayer" or "occasional prayer".

---

### 24-hour prayer coverage

- **Meaning**: The goal of having at least 144 intercessors each praying
  10 minutes a day for a specific UUPG, which collectively adds up to 24 hours
  of prayer daily.
- **Example**: "At least 144 intercessors praying for each UUPG 10 minutes a
  day (which equals 24-hour prayer coverage)" (Vision page)
- **Why it matters**: The mathematical logic must be translatable. Avoid
  "coverage" being read as insurance or legal coverage; it means *uninterrupted
  prayer throughout the day*.

---

### Intercessor / intercessors

- **Meaning**: A person who commits to pray on behalf of a UUPG.
  Goal: 144+ per UUPG (for 24-hour coverage), then 1,000+ per UUPG.
- **Example**: "Raise up 144+ daily intercessors to pray 10 minutes a day."
  (Adopt page)
- **Why it matters**: Use the established church term for intercessor/prayer
  warrior, not a courtroom or governmental connotation.

---

### Intercessory prayer

- **Meaning**: Prayer offered on behalf of others — specifically for people
  groups who have no one praying for them.
- **Example**: "Broader engagement includes… intercessory prayer" (Definitions)
- **Why it matters**: Distinguish from personal devotional prayer; emphasize
  the *on behalf of others* dimension.

---

### Daily prayer guide / daily prayer points

- **Meaning**: The daily email or content resource that Doxa.Life sends to
  registered intercessors — including Scripture themes, prayer needs from the
  field, photos, stories, and key insights about the people group.
- **Example**: "Receive daily prayer points and join believers worldwide in
  prayer" (homepage); "Your daily prayer guide: Scripture-centered prayer
  themes, Spirit-led reflection and guidance, Real prayer needs from the field,
  Photos, stories and testimonies, Key insights about the people group" (Pray)
- **Why it matters**: The "guide" and "points" are distinct from general
  devotionals — they are specific, people-group-focused, field-connected.

---

### Prayer prepares the way

- **Meaning**: A theological conviction that intercession creates spiritual
  conditions for gospel breakthroughs before workers even arrive.
- **Example**: "Prayer softens hearts, opens doors, and invites the work of
  the Holy Spirit long before workers arrive or the gospel is proclaimed."
  (Pray page)
- **Why it matters**: A key motivational phrase used to explain why prayer is
  the first step of engagement, not just a supplement to it.

---

### Lord of the harvest

- **Meaning**: A biblical title for Jesus (Matthew 9:38), used in the call to
  pray that He would send workers to the unengaged.
- **Example**: "Pray earnestly to the Lord of the harvest … that He would send
  laborers to the [Unengaged]." (Pray page)
- **Why it matters**: Align with the established Bible translation phrase in
  the target language. The agricultural metaphor (harvest = people ready to
  respond) should carry over.

## 4. Adoption and mobilization

---

### Adopt (an unengaged people group) ★ Seed term

- **Meaning**: A formal, long-term church-led commitment to pray, give, and
  send so that gospel access begins for a specific unengaged people group.
- **Example**: "A church-led commitment to pray, give, and send so that gospel
  access begins" (Adopt page)
- **Why it matters**: Avoid family-law adoption language (children, legal
  custody). The concept is *missional sponsorship/commitment* — a church takes
  strategic responsibility for a people group's engagement.

---

### Pray, give, and send

- **Meaning**: The three pillars of adoption — intercessory prayer, financial
  support, and sending workers.
- **Example**: "Churches and networks take ownership – praying, giving, and
  preparing the way for gospel workers." (homepage); "Partner through prayer,
  giving, and sending" (Adopt page)
- **Why it matters**: This triad is used consistently. All three actions should
  be translated with active, participatory verbs, not passive or devotional
  ones.

---

### Mobilize / mobilization

- **Meaning**: Raising up and activating intercessors and participants across
  a congregation or network.
- **Example**: "Raise up 144+ daily intercessors" / "Tools to mobilize prayer
  and participation across your congregation" (Adopt page)
- **Why it matters**: "Mobilize" implies active movement toward a goal. Avoid
  passive words like "inform" or "invite".

---

### Sending agency / sending agencies

- **Meaning**: Organizations authorized to recruit, train, support, and send
  missionaries. DOXA works with but does not replace sending agencies.
- **Example**: "Every World Assemblies of God Fellowship affiliated missions
  department (129 different sending agencies as of 2026) is automatically a
  DOXA partner." (About page)
- **Why it matters**: A technical term in mission structures. Translators
  should use the established local equivalent if one exists.

---

### Our gift to Jesus

- **Meaning**: The framing metaphor for the mission — engaging every people
  group is the church's collective gift of worship to Jesus.
- **Example**: "Our gift to Jesus: Engage every people by 2033" (homepage)
- **Why it matters**: A motivational/worshipful frame. Should feel sacrificial
  and offering-like, not transactional.

## 6. Church, discipleship, and theological terms

---

### Disciple / disciples (as a metric)

- **Meaning**: Followers of Jesus who are baptized, gathered, and reproducible.
  Used as the unit of measurement for engagement thresholds (e.g., "2 disciples
  or fewer out of 100").
- **Example**: "A church is comprised of baptized disciples joined together by
  the Holy Spirit" (Definitions); "2 disciples or fewer out of 100" (UPG def.)
- **Why it matters**: Use the established Christian word for disciple, not
  "student", "follower" (generic), or "convert". The numeric metric usage must
  remain connected to this theological meaning.

---

### Baptized disciples

- **Meaning**: The formal definition of church membership on Doxa.Life —
  people who have followed Jesus in believer's baptism and are joined together
  by the Holy Spirit.
- **Example**: "A church is comprised of baptized disciples joined together by
  the Holy Spirit" (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: "Baptized" is a qualifier that distinguishes nominal
  affiliation from genuine membership.

---

### Make disciples of all the nations

- **Meaning**: The Great Commission mandate from Matthew 28:19, quoted in the
  Mission section.
- **Example**: "For the glory of God, to make disciples of all the nations by
  going, baptizing, and teaching." (Vision page — Mission)
- **Why it matters**: Align with the trusted local Bible translation. "Nations"
  here means ethnic peoples, not countries.

---

### Culturally appropriate and locally relevant

- **Meaning**: One of the three criteria for a people group being considered
  "engaged" — that the gospel work fits the culture and language of the people.
- **Example**: "The work occurs in culturally appropriate and locally relevant
  ways." (Vision page)
- **Why it matters**: Contextualization language — must convey adaptation to
  culture without implying compromise of the gospel.

---

### Self-sustaining churches

- **Meaning**: Churches that can support themselves financially, reproduce
  themselves, and govern themselves without external dependency.
- **Example**: "There are efforts to establish self-sustaining churches"
  (Vision page — engagement definition)
- **Why it matters**: "Self-sustaining" is distinct from "independent" — it
  implies healthy maturity, not isolation.

---

### Spiritual generations (of churches)

- **Meaning**: The number of "generations" of church-planting that a CPM has
  produced (A plants B, B plants C, C plants D = 4 generations).
- **Example**: "four streams of four spiritual generations" (Definitions page)
- **Why it matters**: A technical CPM metric. Translators should convey
  multiplicative lineage, not age of believers.

---

### Abide in Jesus

- **Meaning**: Core value from John 15:5 — remaining in ongoing, dependent
  relationship with Jesus as the source of all fruitfulness.
- **Example**: "Abide in Jesus (John 15:5)" (Vision page — Values)
- **Why it matters**: "Abide" carries a relational, continuous sense. Avoid
  rendering as a single past act or a physical location.

---

### Pray without ceasing

- **Meaning**: Core value from 1 Thessalonians 5:17 — the posture of ongoing,
  habitual prayer underlying all of Doxa.Life's prayer vision.
- **Example**: "Pray without ceasing (1 Thessalonians 5:17)" (Vision page)
- **Why it matters**: Use the established Bible translation phrase. Emphasize
  continuity, not literal impossibility.

---

### Deny yourself

- **Meaning**: Core value from Luke 9:23 — voluntary self-sacrifice as
  disciples follow Jesus into mission.
- **Example**: "Deny yourself (Luke 9:23)" (Vision page — Values)
- **Why it matters**: Convey willing self-renunciation for Christ's sake, not
  self-hatred or asceticism.

---

### Engage the regions beyond

- **Meaning**: Core value from 2 Corinthians 10:16 — reaching beyond where the
  gospel has already gone.
- **Example**: "Engage the regions beyond (2 Corinthians 10:16)" (Vision page)
- **Why it matters**: "Beyond" signals frontier/pioneer zones. Link with the
  frontier people group concept.

## 7. Organizational and structural terms

  but do not translate it.

---

- **Example**: "DOXA was started and is stewarded by the World Assemblies of

- **Why it matters**: A proper name — do not translate. Add a brief

### WAGF Missions Commission

- **Meaning**: The oversight body within WAGF that appoints and oversees

- **Why it matters**: A proper institutional name; do not translate.

---

### Sending Summit Declaration

- **Example**: "SENDING SUMMIT DECLARATION" (About page heading)
- **Why it matters**: A proper-noun document name; keep as is or add a brief

---

### Voluntary, Global, Receiving Partnership

### Statement of Faith

- **Meaning**: The doctrinal summary of WAGF convictions, listed on
  doxa.life/about/statement-of-faith.
- **Why it matters**: Use the established church term for "doctrinal statement"
  or "confession of faith" in the target language. Avoid legal-sounding words.

---

### Pentecostal

- **Meaning**: The theological tradition and distinctive of WAGF/Assemblies of
  God, as stated in the Statement of Faith introduction.
- **Example**: "The truth set forth is held to be essential to a truly

- **Why it matters**: A proper theological designation — keep as "Pentecostal"

## 8. Statement of Faith terms (theological)

These terms appear specifically in the Statement of Faith page. They are
standard doctrinal vocabulary. Translators should use established Christian
equivalents in the target language rather than inventing new translations.

| Term | Brief note |
|------|-----------|
| **New birth** | Conversion/regeneration — being "born again" (John 3:5–6) |
| **Regeneration** | The Holy Spirit's work of spiritual renewal at salvation |
| **Justification** | Being declared righteous before God through faith in Christ |
| **Atonement** | Christ's substitutionary death covering humanity's sin |
| **Sanctification** | Ongoing separation from evil and dedication to God |
| **Baptism in water** | Believer's baptism by immersion (Matthew 28:19; Acts 10:47–48) |
| **Lord's Supper / Communion** | The ordinance of bread and cup (Luke 22:14–20) |
| **Baptism in the Holy Spirit** | A distinct experience of empowerment, evidenced by tongues |
| **Speaking in tongues** | The initial evidence of baptism in the Holy Spirit |
| **Gifts of the Holy Spirit** | Supernatural gifts (1 Corinthians 12) |
| **Pre-millennial return** | Christ's imminent, personal, bodily return before the millennium |
| **Bodily resurrection** | Physical resurrection of all humanity at the end of time |
